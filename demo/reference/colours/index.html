Brand colours change from project to project and will always be provided by a designer or the client's brand guidelines. The tertiary colour is optional but highly recommended for active states and more variations.

A palette of three brand colours each with two darker and two lighter tones provides a balance of flexibility in the design with reduced complexity and development time in the build.

Avoid using black or white as primary colours when there is a choice as this creates unecessary duplication with the below predefined colours.

Predefined Colours

These colours do not change on a per-project basis, this provides consistency for designers and developers and reduced project time.

The notification colours have been carefully chosen to conform to AA accessibility colour contrast requirements. While the black and white tints exist to provide a slightly less harsh option.
